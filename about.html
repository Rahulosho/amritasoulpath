Childhood of Survival

Amrita was born in South America, into a life that demanded strength. Growing up in poverty, she navigated her youth in survival mode, shouldering responsibilities well beyond her years. At 16, her family immigrated to Canada. She worked full-time while attending college at night. Her youth was resilience defined.

Creating a Beautiful Cage

By 22, Amrita was married. By 26, she had two children and a life of material stability. Yet beneath the surface, she felt a deep ache. She built a business — CurveAppeal Personal Training — and coached thousands. But she was pouring from an empty cup.

When It All Fell Apart

The pandemic crushed her business. Then she lost her sister, niece, and father. Her world collapsed. For the first time, she had nothing left — but in that void, a whisper rose: “This is not the end. This is the beginning.”

From Grind to Grace

At 47, Amrita left Canada with just a carry-on. Her journey took her through Thailand, Bali, and India — through yoga, silence, grief, and awakening. Each place stripped layers away until only her soul remained, raw and real.

The Birth of AmritaSoulPath

In Rishikesh, she completed over 500 hours of yogic training. What emerged wasn’t a business — it was remembrance. Now she guides others through retreats, coaching, meditation, and sacred healing, walking beside them on their own soul paths.
